Our Animals

At the zoo, we are committed to the welfare of our animals and the preservation of their natural habitats. Our animals have plenty of space to roam around in and are well-cared for by our experienced staff. In addition to our animal exhibits, we offer a wide variety of educational programs and events for all ages, including behind-the-scenes tours, animal encounters, and special exhibits.

We believe that by educating our visitors about the importance of conservation and the role that zoos can play in protecting endangered species, we can inspire the next generation of animal lovers and conservationists. We work closely with other organizations around the world to support conservation efforts and promote sustainable practices. When you visit our zoo, you'll have the opportunity to see a wide range of animals up close and learn more about their behaviors, diets, and habitats.

We invite you to plan your visit today and experience the wonder of the animal kingdom firsthand. Whether you're looking to spend a day with your family, learn something new about your favorite animals, or support a worthy cause, the zoo has something for everyone. Come see us soon and join us in our mission to protect and preserve the world's wildlife.

Our Map

The Lambton Zoo is set in the beautiful Rouge Valley. The Main Entrance is located on Meadowvale Road, Lambton, north of Highway 401 (Exit #389 eastbound and westbound). The Zoo is accessible by car and public transit. Parking is $15.00. EV Charging stations are available in Section C of our main parking lot. The Flo Level 2 stations are operational using a credit card, a Flo card or through the Flo app. Visit https://www.flo.com/en-CA/drivers/network/ to learn more about using the EV charging stations. Charging stations are on a first come, first serve basis. Once your vehicle has been charged, we ask that guests please move their vehicles to allow others to access them.

Address: 2000 Meadowvale Road, Lambton, Ontario, M1B 5K7
